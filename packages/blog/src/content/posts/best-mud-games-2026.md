MUDs (Multi-User Dungeons) are text-based multiplayer RPGs that have been running since the late 1970s. Despite their age, dozens of MUDs are still thriving in 2026 with active communities, regular updates, and gameplay depth that many modern MMOs can't match. Here are the best ones worth playing right now.

## 1. Ultimate Dominion

**Type:** Browser-based MUD | **Cost:** Free | **Platform:** Any web browser

[Ultimate Dominion](https://ultimatedominion.com) is a free multiplayer text-based RPG playable in any browser. Fight monsters, trade items, and build your legacy in a persistent on-chain world on Base. No downloads required.

**What makes it stand out:**
- Runs entirely in the browser — no downloads, no telnet clients, no setup
- Turn-based combat with a STR/AGI/INT combat triangle
- 9 advanced classes with unique abilities
- Player-driven marketplace with on-chain item trading
- PvP with gold escrow in danger zones
- Every character, item, and piece of gold exists permanently on the blockchain
- Embedded wallet — no crypto knowledge needed

**Best for:** Players who want a modern MUD experience without any technical setup. Good entry point for people who've never tried a MUD before.

**Start playing:** [ultimatedominion.com](https://ultimatedominion.com)

## 2. Achaea, Dreams of Divine Lands

**Type:** Traditional MUD (web client available) | **Cost:** Free with optional purchases | **Platform:** Web client, Mudlet, other telnet clients

Achaea is one of the most well-known commercial MUDs, running since 1997. It's a fantasy MUD with deep combat, political systems, player-run cities, and a rich mythology maintained by active game masters.

**What makes it stand out:**
- Massive, detailed world developed over 25+ years
- Complex PvP combat with hundreds of abilities
- Player-run governments, religions, and organizations
- Active events run by dedicated game masters
- Polished web client for browser play

**Best for:** Experienced MUD players who want deep PvP combat and political roleplay. Steep learning curve.

## 3. Aardwolf

**Type:** Traditional hack-and-slash MUD | **Cost:** Free | **Platform:** Mudlet, telnet clients

Aardwolf is a classic hack-and-slash MUD that's been running since 1996. It's one of the most popular MUDs for pure leveling and combat, with a "remort" system that lets you restart with bonuses after reaching max level.

**What makes it stand out:**
- Deep leveling system with 7 tiers of remort progression
- Hundreds of zones and thousands of rooms
- Custom Mudlet plugin with a built-in mapper
- Active playerbase and helpful community
- Great for grinding and character optimization

**Best for:** Players who love leveling, character builds, and pure hack-and-slash gameplay.

## 4. Alter Aeon

**Type:** Traditional MUD with web client | **Cost:** Free | **Platform:** Custom client, Mudlet, web client

Alter Aeon has been running since 1995 and is notable for its multi-class system. Instead of picking one class, you level all classes simultaneously, creating hybrid builds.

**What makes it stand out:**
- Multi-class system (Warrior, Thief, Mage, Cleric, Necromancer, Druid)
- Extensive crafting system
- Built-in accessibility features for visually impaired players
- Active development with regular content updates
- Custom client with sound effects and music

**Best for:** Players who want deep character customization and a solo-friendly experience.

## 5. Discworld MUD

**Type:** Themed MUD | **Cost:** Free | **Platform:** Mudlet, telnet clients

Based on Terry Pratchett's Discworld novels, this MUD captures the humor and world of the books. It's been running since 1991 and has one of the most unique settings in the MUD world.

**What makes it stand out:**
- Skill-based progression (no classes or levels)
- Enormous world based on Discworld lore
- Player-run guilds with unique abilities
- Witty writing true to Pratchett's style
- One of the largest active MUD communities

**Best for:** Terry Pratchett fans and players who prefer skill-based over class-based systems.

## 6. Armageddon MUD

**Type:** Roleplay-enforced MUD | **Cost:** Free | **Platform:** Mudlet, telnet clients

Armageddon is a hardcore roleplay-enforced MUD set in a brutal desert world. Permadeath, mandatory in-character behavior, and a harsh survival environment make it one of the most intense MUD experiences available.

**What makes it stand out:**
- Mandatory roleplay — all communication must be in-character
- Permadeath — when you die, that character is gone forever
- Complex crafting, foraging, and survival systems
- Staff-driven plots and world events
- Incredibly immersive atmosphere

**Best for:** Experienced roleplayers who want a hardcore, immersive experience. Not for casual play.

## 7. Sindome

**Type:** Cyberpunk roleplay MUD | **Cost:** Free | **Platform:** Web client, telnet

Sindome is a cyberpunk-themed roleplay MUD set in a dystopian future. Think Blade Runner meets Snow Crash in text form. Player-driven economy, faction warfare, and permadeath keep stakes high.

**What makes it stand out:**
- Cyberpunk setting with corporate intrigue
- Player-driven economy and faction politics
- Roleplay-focused with strong community moderation
- Web-based client available
- Active development and regular events

**Best for:** Players who want roleplay in a sci-fi setting rather than fantasy.

## How to Choose

- **Never played a MUD before?** Start with [Ultimate Dominion](https://ultimatedominion.com) — it's browser-based, free, and has a [complete guide](https://ultimatedominion.com/guide). No setup required.
- **Want deep PvP combat?** Achaea has the most developed PvP system in the MUD genre.
- **Love leveling and grinding?** Aardwolf's remort system gives you endless progression.
- **Hardcore roleplayer?** Armageddon (fantasy) or Sindome (cyberpunk) for full immersion.
- **Terry Pratchett fan?** Discworld MUD is the obvious choice.
- **Want on-chain ownership?** Ultimate Dominion stores everything on the blockchain — you provably own your characters and items.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Are these MUDs all still active?**
Yes. Every MUD listed here has an active playerbase and receives regular updates as of March 2026.

**Which MUD has the most players?**
Player counts fluctuate, but Achaea and Aardwolf typically have the largest concurrent playerbases among traditional MUDs. Ultimate Dominion is growing as a browser-based alternative.

**Do I need a special program to play?**
For browser-based MUDs like Ultimate Dominion, no — just open the website. For traditional MUDs, Mudlet is the most popular free client.

**Can I play MUDs on mobile?**
Browser-based MUDs like Ultimate Dominion work on mobile browsers. Traditional MUDs can be played through mobile telnet apps, though the experience varies.

**Are MUDs pay-to-win?**
Most MUDs are completely free with no pay-to-win mechanics. Achaea offers optional purchases that can provide advantages. Ultimate Dominion and the others on this list are free.
