UX Concept

Gold Merchant — Fiat Onramp

How non-crypto players acquire gold without ever touching ETH, wallets, or exchanges.

01

The Friction Point

A non-crypto player reaches level 5, sees a Rare Sword on the marketplace for 500 gold, but only has 80. They could grind for hours — or they'd happily pay $5 for the gold right now. But the current path is:

Player wants 500 Gold → Find a crypto exchange (Coinbase?) → Create account, KYC, wait 3 days → Buy ETH with credit card → Bridge ETH to Base network (?) → Find the GOLD/ETH pool on Uniswap (?) → Swap ETH for GOLD → Player has Gold
⚠
Every invisible step is a dropout point. Most non-crypto players will never complete this flow. They'll either grind (and maybe churn), or just leave. The game loses revenue and the player loses fun.
02

The Ideal Flow

With Thirdweb Universal Bridge (already in your SDK), the entire chain collapses to one step:

Player taps "Get Gold" → Enters amount: 500 Gold → Sees price: ~$5.55 → Pays with credit card / Apple Pay → (Thirdweb: fiat → ETH on Base → 10% fee to company → GOLD via Uniswap) → Gold appears in inventory. Done.
✨
The player never sees ETH, Base, Uniswap, or any crypto concept. They buy gold the same way they'd buy gems in Clash of Clans. Thirdweb handles KYC, payment processing, routing, and the swap — all behind a single embedded UI.
03

Provider Options

Multiple services can power the "credit card → gold" flow. Since UD already uses Thirdweb SDK v5, that's the lowest-friction integration.

Provider How It Works Pros Cons
Thirdweb Pay REC PayEmbed component. Fiat → ETH → GOLD routed automatically via Universal Bridge. Embeds directly in game UI. Already in your SDK. Dark theme. Handles KYC. Apple Pay, Google Pay, cards. 160+ countries. Requires DEX liquidity for GOLD routing. Onramp fees ~2-5%.
Stripe Onramp ALT Embeddable widget. Fiat → ETH on Base. Requires separate swap step for GOLD. Trusted brand. Low fraud. Great conversion rates. Only gets player to ETH — still need a swap step. US + EU only.
MoonPay ALT White-label onramp. Fiat → ETH. Can customize branding. Broad country coverage. White-label option. Higher fees (~4.5%). Separate integration. ETH only, not GOLD.
Direct Sale ALT Protocol sells GOLD directly from a funded wallet. Player pays fiat, protocol transfers GOLD. Simplest UX. Fixed pricing. No DEX dependency. Requires maintaining a gold inventory. Regulatory complexity (selling a token directly).
🎯
Recommendation: Start with Thirdweb Pay. It's already in the SDK, handles the full fiat-to-GOLD pipeline (including the Uniswap swap), and can be themed to match the game. If Thirdweb's routing can't find your GOLD token (low DEX liquidity), fall back to fiat → ETH with an auto-swap on arrival.
04

Fee Structure & Revenue

The company takes a 10% fee in ETH, deducted after the fiat onramp and before the Uniswap swap. This is a configurable parameter via Thirdweb's developer fee — no custom contract needed.

Player pays $5.55 → Stripe processes card (~2.5% fee → Stripe keeps ~$0.14) → $5.41 of ETH lands on Base → 10% fee → $0.50 ETH to company wallet → $4.91 ETH swaps for GOLD on Uniswap (1% pool fee → LPs) → ~486 Gold arrives in player's balance

💰
Why 10% and why in ETH: Starting high lets you lower the fee later as a goodwill gesture once demand is proven sticky — you can never raise a fee without backlash. ETH is immediately liquid for operations, relayer top-ups, and pool seeding. Taking the fee in GOLD would create sell pressure on your own token.
📊
Industry context: ~13.5% total is well below mobile gaming norms. Apple/Google take 30% on in-app purchases. Fortnite direct takes ~12%. Players are accustomed to paying a premium for convenience. The key is that the player sees one total price ("$5.55 for 500 Gold") — they don't see the fee breakdown unless they look for it.

Design Decisions

  • No crypto language. "Gold Merchant" not "Swap" or "Onramp." "Purchase" not "Bridge." "Card" not "Fiat."
  • Gold-denominated. Player enters how much gold they want, not how much USD to spend. The game sets the frame.
  • Quick amounts anchor expectations and reduce decision fatigue. Pre-set to common purchase sizes.
  • Price shown in USD. Player sees the real cost. No hidden fees. "Service fee" bundles the 10% company fee + processing — player doesn't need to know the breakdown.
  • Total on the button. The buy button shows the exact charge. No surprises after clicking. Builds trust.
  • Crypto option exists but isn't prominent. Power users who want to pay with existing ETH can, but it's not the default.
  • Same modal chrome as existing game modals (clip-path corners, gold header, dark card bg). Feels native, not third-party.
  • Market rate disclaimer manages expectations around price volatility without using terms like "slippage" or "AMM."

Implementation Path

Rough ordering based on impact and effort.

Phase What Effort Notes
Phase 1 Add Thirdweb PayEmbed to a "Gold Merchant" modal. Wire it to buy GOLD on Base. Theme to match game UI. 1-2 days Requires GOLD to be routable via Universal Bridge (needs DEX liquidity). Test on beta pool first.
Phase 2 Add contextual triggers: "+" on stats panel, "Get Gold" on shop/marketplace insufficient-gold errors, enhance OutOfResourcesModal. Half day These are small UI additions to existing components.
Phase 3 Pre-fill amounts from context. "You need 268 more gold" → opens merchant with 268 pre-filled. Half day Reduces friction further. Player sees exact cost to unblock themselves.
Phase 4 Analytics: track funnel (trigger shown → modal opened → purchase started → purchase completed). Optimize placement. 1 day Essential for knowing which trigger points convert and which don't.
Future Gold offramp (sell gold for fiat). Player cashout flow. "Sell to Gold Merchant" button. TBD Requires offramp provider (Thirdweb doesn't offer this yet). Lower priority — earning gold should feel rewarding, not like earning fiat.
🛡
Manifesto check — "Fair economics." This doesn't violate "money can't buy insurmountable advantage" because gold buys items that are also earnable through gameplay. A whale can accelerate but can't access anything a grinder can't. The marketplace already enables this dynamic — the onramp just removes the crypto friction from it.
